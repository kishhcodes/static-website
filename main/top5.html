Home Records My Top 5 favs

1. Lust for Life

“Lust for Life” isn’t just a song—it’s a cosmic sigh of rebellion, romance, and raw desire. There’s something otherworldly about hearing Lana Del Rey and The Weeknd duet at the edge of the Hollywood sign, dangling their feet off the metaphorical cliff of fame and innocence.


Lyric snippet:

They say the only the Good die young, but that just ain't right.

2. Diet Mountain Dew

Toxic But Sweet. At its core, “Diet Mountain Dew” is a flirtation with toxicity. Lana sings like she’s sipping from a chalice of heartbreak and pop art fantasy, and she likes the taste.


Lyric snippet:

Never was there ever a girl so pretty.

3. West Coast

DAMN. “West Coast” does something few mainstream singles dare: it destabilizes you. It starts like a sultry rock track—throbbing with desire—and then suddenly, the beat slows down, and you’re left floating in molasses. Then it snaps back. Then it drifts again.


Lyric snippet:

Down on the West coast, they got a sayin' "If you're not drinkin', then you're not playin'"

4. White Mustang

a song that blends cool detachment with tragic intimacy. It may clock in under 3 minutes, but it contains an entire relationship arc: from glittery obsession to inevitable disappointment, all wrapped in a haze of synths, sighs, and west coast melancholy.


Lyric snippet:

Packing all my things for the summer Lying on my bed, it's a bummer

5. Young and Beautiful

“Will you still love me when I'm no longer young and beautiful?” This line hits like marble at first—cold, smooth, elegant—but then you realize it’s a desperate plea. Lana isn't just asking; she's praying. It’s in that quiet vulnerability that she becomes her most cinematic and emotionally exposed.


Lyric snippet:

They say the only the Good die young, but that just ain't right.
